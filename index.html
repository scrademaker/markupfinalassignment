About Haarlem

Haarlem in a nutshell

A cozy and historic center, famous museums, shops, restaurants and the beach around the corner: Welcome to Haarlem, the city that has everything.

Haarlem is a city and a municipality in The Netherlands and the capital of the province North-Holland. The city is located at the river the Spaarne which is in the region of South-Kennemerland. Haarlem is one of the medium-sized cities in the Randstad. The municipality of Haarlem includes the city of Haarlem and the western part of the village Spaarndam.

From hidden courtyards from back in the days, to modern concept stores. From medieval church to waterfront terrace. From Dutch masters to French star chefs. From antique market to pop concert. Fancy an unforgettable experience? Visit Haarlem and be surprised by the sights, boutiques and picturesque squares, by the old and contemporary artists, the Burgundian atmosphere and the rich history.

What Haarlem has to offer

Seeking for delicious food and drinks? Then Haarlem is the place to be. The city is known for its varied culinary offer: from hip coffee bars and excellent lunchrooms to cozy cocktail places and special restaurants, from simple to exclusive. In the summer you can settle down on one of the many terraces or the city beach. Would you rather enjoy a real Michelin star restaurant? That is also possible in Haarlem, the most Burgundian city of the North.

Haarlem is easily accessible by car and public transportation. Haarlem has two train stations, an excellent bus connection with Schiphol and can be easily reached from Amsterdam, Utrecht and The Hague. At the access roads of the city you will find directions for the public parking garages; there is little street parking in the center. Bicycles can be parked for free in one of the guarded and covered bicycle parking areas. Haarlem is surrounded by beautiful nature and recreation areas where you can enjoy cycling. Rent a bike at one of the bike rental locations in and around the city and find out yourself! Haarlem is easily accessible by public transport. Haarlem has two train stations, a bus station and various bus stops. You can easily and quickly plan your trip by public transportation via the website/app of 9292.

Marvel at the treasures of science, go back to the Golden Age or surround yourself with contemporary art. With more that 10 different museums, Haarlem offers art and culture for everyone. The Frans Hals and Teylers Museum are internationally known and show high-profile exhibitions. Het Dolhuys takes you into the world of the spirits. There is also plenty to discover in the museums for children. Haarlem is a living city. There are festivals and events for young and old. In addition to activities in museums and venues, there are many annual events such as the Bloemenweekend, Bevrijdingspop, Haarlem Culinair and Haarlem Jazz & More. The kick-off of the cultural season takes place on the second weekend of September. October is children-month in Haarlem and traditionally the atmospheric Christmas market takes place in December. See what else is on the agenda and enjoy!

Hwa-Rang Dragon - Taekwondo

Hwa-Rang Dragon - Taekwondo is located in the neighbourhood Schalkwijk in Haarlem. They offer Taekwondo practice for kids and adults.

Taekwondo is originally a Korean martial arts where both hand and foot techniques are used. The literal translation of the word Taekwondo is: "The way of the foot and the fist." The word Taekwondo is made out of three parts:

Tae (foot): The foot represents all leg techniques such as kicking and jumping.

Kwon (fist): The hand stands for all hand and arm techniques such as bumping, blocks, gripping, clamping and throwing.

Do (The path): The road is the path that you follow while practicing Taekwondo and mainly represents the mental part.

Sportclubs in Haarlem

Olympia Haarlem has a variety of sports to offer: Soccer, baseball, badminton, billiards and darts. They have 3 soccer fields and 1 baseball field.

United DAVO offers soccer and baseball practice for all ages. With 2 soccer fields and 1 baseball field there is enough room for everyone.

Gym Haarlem is one of the most popular gyms in Haarlem. They offer kickboxing, thaiboxing, fitness, joga, MMA and more.

Smile Sport, the coziest gym of Haarlem. They offer personal training, massages and crossfit.

Royal row and sail club is located next to The Spaarne, a river where rowing and sailing is practiced.

Ryo Un Kai is a karate club that focusses on the body and the spirit.

AikiDojo is the name of one of the aikido club in Haarlem. "Aikido is not a technique to fight with or defeat the enemy. It is a way to reconcile the world and make human beings one family." - Morihei Ueshiba

Hwa-Rang Dragonhas a variety of martial arts to offer such as Taekwondo, mudo, Brazilian jiu jitsu and FightFit.

HBSis a hockey club located in Bloemendaal, a village next to Haarlem. They offer hockey practice for all ages.

SV De Blinkert offers handball practice for all ages and for disabled persons since 1954.
